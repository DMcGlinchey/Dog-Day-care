Exercise

Like human dogs need exercise to burn calories, stimulate their mind to stay healthy. Exercise can vary depending on the dog’s breed, sex, age and level of health. Small dogs may only require 1 to 2 walks a day while larger or working dogs would require significant more and for longer durations. Different types of dogs have different exercise requirements to maintain their optimal health. It is essential for a veterinarian and owner to understand the dog-related characteristics of exercise. Understanding the dog will allow them to understand the dog’s behaviour toward exercise, allowing a strategy to implemented to prevent obesity. Furthermore, encouraging regular exercise helps to improve the health of the dog.







Diet

A dog’s diet is important, good diets allows the animal stray fit and healthy. Over or under feeding can cause health problems and prevent the animal form being so active. Balanced diets help to maintain cells, growth and overall health. Food pacifically made for dog is high-quality and contains nutrients they need. These meals can differ depending on the dogs age, once a dog starts eating it will require four meals a day up until it of 12 weeks. The amount of meal will keep shrinking up until the dog has reach it first birthday where it will receive one meal a day. The portions size will differ on the size of the dog and breed, working dogs require large portion size, as they are constantly moving. Premium -quality dry food provides a well-balanced diet for adult dogs, providing vitamins and nutrients they may need. Theses dish would need water or canned food to allow enough water into their diet.

Medical

Dogs should receive regular medical care, as a human would. Veterinarians provide information on keep animals healthy and provide a vaccination schedule, deworming and external parasites. A veterinary is there to keep an animal safe and healthy, providing the owner with further information. When attending business as day care they may request that all dogs have been neutered before attending. The operation would take place at a vet. Usually while the dog is young, once they are between 6 to 9 months old, depending on the breed and gender. Dog’s should not be given any medication that hasn’t been prescribed by a veterinarian. This may not include dental products that can be purchased in store, as they provide nutarians. Unprescribed medicine could cause more harm to the animal and give it unknown side effects.
